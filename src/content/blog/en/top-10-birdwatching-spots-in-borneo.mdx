Borneo is one of the most biologically extraordinary places on Earth. The third-largest island in the world supports more than 620 bird species, including over 50 endemics found nowhere else — among them the Bornean Bristlehead, the Bornean Ground-cuckoo, and eight endemic hornbill species. The island's forests, from lowland dipterocarp rainforest to montane moss forest, are among the most species-rich habitats in the world, and the combination of an accessible tourist infrastructure in the Malaysian states of Sabah and Sarawak and an extraordinarily rich endemic avifauna makes Borneo one of the premier birdwatching destinations in Asia. ## 1. Kinabatangan Wildlife Sanctuary, Sabah

The lower Kinabatangan River in Sabah is Borneo's most wildlife-rich accessible habitat, a corridor of gallery forest alongside one of the longest rivers on the island. Eight hornbill species occur here, including the Rhinoceros Hornbill, Wrinkled Hornbill, and Oriental Pied Hornbill. Storm's Stork is among the rarest resident waterbirds; Wallace's Hawk-Eagle, Bornean Bristlehead, and multiple kingfisher species are regularly seen from boat rides along the river. The Bilit and Sukau areas provide lodges with direct river access, and early morning boat trips during low water periods offer outstanding birding.

## 2. Danum Valley Conservation Area, Sabah

Danum Valley is among the best-preserved areas of lowland dipterocarp forest remaining in Borneo, and its Field Studies Centre provides research-quality access to the forest interior. The Bornean Ground-cuckoo, arguably Borneo's most sought-after endemic, has been recorded regularly on the trails around the field centre. Bornean Peacock-Pheasant, Bornean Keeled Pittas, and the full suite of lowland forest species occur here. Access is via Lahad Datu, and visitor numbers are restricted to maintain the ecological integrity of the site.

## 3. Gunung Kinabalu National Park, Sabah

The forests around Mount Kinabalu — Borneo's highest peak at 4,095 metres — hold an exceptional concentration of montane endemics. Kinabalu Friendly Warbler, Bornean Stubtail, Mountain Blackeye, and the spectacular Kinabalu Serpent-Eagle are restricted to the montane forests of this mountain or closely adjacent ranges. The Kinabalu Park headquarters area provides access to trails through lower and mid-montane forest; the route to the summit passes through upper montane and subalpine zones with additional specialists.

## 4. Tawau Hills Park, Sabah

Tawau Hills Park protects a block of foothill and lower montane forest in the southeastern corner of Sabah. The park is less visited than Danum or Kinabalu but holds several Bornean endemics and is particularly good for forest floor species including pittas. The Bornean Ground-cuckoo has been recorded here, and the hill forest is productive for barbets, woodpeckers, and forest kingfishers.

## 5. Tabin Wildlife Reserve, Sabah

Tabin Wildlife Reserve is primarily known for its Sumatran Rhino population, but the lowland dipterocarp forest and secondary growth around the mud volcanoes within the reserve hold good populations of forest birds, including Bornean species not easily found elsewhere. The reserve's mix of primary forest and edge habitat supports a wider range of species than pure primary forest.

## 6. Gunung Mulu National Park, Sarawak

Gunung Mulu National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the interior of Sarawak, combines extraordinary cave systems with some of the most intact lowland and montane forest in Malaysian Borneo. The park holds populations of Bornean endemics including Hose's Broadbill, Black Oriole, and several montane specialists accessible via the park's trail system. The Deer Cave and Clearwater Cave complex are also worth visiting for their dramatic bat exits at dusk.

## 7. Crocker Range, Sabah

The Crocker Range running through central Sabah provides a largely accessible transect through montane forest with excellent birding along the cross-Sabah road from Kota Kinabalu to Tambunan. Fruiting trees along the road attract Mountain Imperial Pigeon, multiple barbet species, and the Bornean Treepie. The Crocker Range Biosphere Reserve encompasses a large area of the range and provides a framework for community-based ecotourism.

## 8. Klias Wetlands, Sabah

The Klias Peninsula on the west coast of Sabah holds mangrove and nipah swamp habitat that supports populations of species not easily found elsewhere in Sabah. The Proboscis Monkey is the flagship mammal species, but the birds of the mangroves — Mangrove Robin, Copper-throated Sunbird, Mangrove Whistler, Mangrove Blue Flycatcher — are excellent, and the area is one of the best in Borneo for mangrove specialties. Firefly tours in the evening along the tidal rivers provide a striking additional experience.

## 9. Bako National Park, Sarawak

Bako National Park near Kuching in Sarawak is a small park on a peninsula but holds a remarkably complete range of coastal and forest habitats including heath forest, mangrove, and beachfront. Bornean Bristlehead has been reliably recorded here, an extraordinary tick for a species otherwise confined to the deep interior. Rufous-collared Kingfisher, Bornean Falconet, and numerous other forest species occur alongside easily seen Proboscis Monkeys.

## 10. Maliau Basin Conservation Area, Sabah

Maliau Basin is one of the most remote and undisturbed areas of primary forest in Borneo, an uplifted plateau in southern Sabah described as Sabah's Lost World. The birding on the access trails and within the basin itself is exceptional for primary forest species, with Bornean Ground-cuckoo, all the major hornbills, and numerous other Bornean endemics recorded by visiting researchers and birders. Access requires advance planning and logistics support but is becoming increasingly available through specialist birding tour operators.
