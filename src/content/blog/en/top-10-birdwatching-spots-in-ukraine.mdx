Ukraine is geographically the largest country lying entirely within Europe, and its size encompasses an extraordinary range of habitats. The Ukrainian Carpathians in the west carry forest systems continuous with those of Poland and Slovakia, with Brown Bear, Lynx, and the full central European forest bird community. The vast steppe grasslands of central and eastern Ukraine are among the last remaining areas of natural temperate grassland in Europe, with breeding Great Bustard and other steppe specialists. The Black Sea coast and the extensive floodplains of the Dnipro, Dniester, and Danube rivers add wetland communities that rank among the most important in all of Europe. The Danube Delta, though primarily in Romania, extends into Ukraine at its northern Kiliya Branch, and the Ukrainian section protects breeding Dalmatian Pelican and Pygmy Cormorant.

Ukraine has produced a remarkable ornithological record with over 400 breeding species documented before recent conflict significantly complicated fieldwork in eastern areas. Access to the western Carpathians, the Danube region, and parts of the central steppe remains feasible and birding is productive. The following ten sites represent the most ornithologically significant locations currently accessible.

## 1. Askania-Nova Biosphere Reserve, Kherson Oblast

Askania-Nova in the southern Ukrainian steppe is unique in Europe: a protected area of natural steppe grassland where Great Bustard, Little Bustard, and Demoiselle Crane all breed. The reserve has operated as a protected area since the 19th century, making it one of the oldest nature reserves on the continent. The ungrazed steppe here is home to Large-headed Locustella, Ruffe's Warbler, and Sociable Lapwing, the last of which uses the short-grass areas in small numbers. Saker Falcon is a resident raptor, hunting the small mammals that populate the open grassland. The reserve's large animal park has historically been used for wildlife introductions but the natural steppe section is the ornithological focus.

## 2. Danube Biosphere Reserve, Odessa Oblast

The Ukrainian portion of the Danube Delta, centered on the Kiliya Branch at the Romanian border, is managed as the Danube Biosphere Reserve and holds breeding colonies of Dalmatian Pelican, Great White Pelican, and the largest known colony of Pygmy Cormorant in the world. The reserve's interior channels are accessible by boat from Vylkove, a town sometimes called the Ukrainian Venice because of its canal streets. The reed beds support all the typical delta species: Great Reed Warbler, Moustached Warbler, Aquatic Warbler, and Great Bittern. White-tailed Eagle is a common presence, and Marsh Harrier hunts the reed beds in all seasons.

## 3. Shatsky National Park, Volyn Oblast

The Shatsky Lakes in northwestern Ukraine near the Belarus and Polish borders form one of the most extensive lake systems in Eastern Europe, a chain of glacial lakes in flat forested landscape. The forests around the lakes hold Middle Spotted Woodpecker, White-backed Woodpecker, and Black Stork nesting in the undisturbed areas. The lake margins support breeding Spotted Crake and Little Crake, both notoriously secretive species that are reliably located here at night by their distinctive calls in May and June. Common Crane breeds in the wet forest clearings. The area is close to Polesia, the vast wetland wilderness spanning Ukraine, Belarus, and Poland, and reflects that region's bird community.

## 4. Polesia and the Pripyat River, Kyiv and Volyn Oblasts

The Polesia region of northern Ukraine, centered on the Pripyat and Desna river floodplains, is a continuation of the vast European wetland complex that extends through Belarus and Poland. The floodplain meadows here hold breeding Great Snipe, a declining wader that performs spectacular communal display flights at dusk in May. Corn Crake is abundant in the unmown meadows and the density of this species in Ukrainian Polesia is among the highest remaining in Europe. Lesser Spotted Eagle and Black Kite are common overhead. Crane migration through Polesia in autumn is impressive, with thousands of birds using the river corridors as flyways.

## 5. Carpathian National Nature Park, Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast

The Ukrainian Carpathians are the easternmost extension of the Carpathian mountain arc and the national park centered on the Gorgany and Chornohora ranges holds intact montane forest systems. Ural Owl is a characteristic species of the mature beech-fir forest and is most easily located in February and March when displaying. Three-toed Woodpecker inhabits the areas of spruce forest with standing deadwood. The higher ridges above the treeline have Upland Pipit, Water Pipit, and Ring Ouzel. Black Stork nests in the forest near streams. The park's extensive trail network, developed primarily for hikers, provides good access to both forest and subalpine habitats.

## 6. Dunaiski National Park, Odessa Oblast

Adjacent to the Danube Biosphere Reserve, the Dunaiski National Park protects additional delta and coastal habitats along the Black Sea coast near the Romanian border. The park's Black Sea coastline includes the Zhebriyanivska Ridges, a series of coastal bluffs overlooking the sea that provide excellent seabird observation points during migration. Yelkouan Shearwater passes in autumn, and Northern Gannet and various skuas occur offshore. The coastal wetlands support breeding Garganey, Ferruginous Duck, and Ruddy Shelduck. The park covers a transition between the Danube floodplain and the Black Sea coastal steppe, and the two habitats give access to completely different bird communities within a short distance.

## 7. Khortytsia Island, Zaporizhzhia

Khortytsia is a large island in the Dnipro River at Zaporizhzhia, a historical Cossack stronghold now managed as a nature reserve within a heavily industrialized city. The island's steppe remnants, riverside willows, and rocky outcrops support a surprisingly diverse bird community given the urban setting. Bee-eater nests in the sandy river banks in colonies visible from the island's edge. Pallid Swift nests in the rock crevices. Eagle Owl breeds on the rocky slopes, and the resident pair can be heard calling in the evenings from the southern cliffs. The island demonstrates how urban nature reserves can maintain populations of species that might otherwise be absent from otherwise developed areas.

## 8. Kryvorizka Steppe Reserve

The steppe reserves of central Ukraine, including the Kryvorizka and related protected areas in Dnipropetrovsk Oblast, protect fragments of the original Ukrainian steppe grassland that once covered most of the country. These reserves hold Little Bustard in declining but persistent numbers, along with breeding Montagu's Harrier, Pallid Harrier, and Stone Curlew. Steppe Eagle is an increasingly rare visitor from its breeding grounds further east. The Short-toed Lark and Calandra Lark are common in open areas. The steppe flora — including feather grass and various wormwoods — creates a distinctive landscape during the summer months that is as ornithologically important as it is botanically unique.

## 9. Tiligulsky Estuary Ramsar Site, Mykolaiv Oblast

The Tiligul estuary is a large coastal lagoon on the Black Sea coast northwest of Odessa, designated as a Ramsar wetland for its importance to waterbirds. The lagoon's shallow margins support nesting Pied Avocet, Black-winged Stilt, and Kentish Plover. Greater Flamingo visits irregularly in spring and autumn, originating from Mediterranean colonies. The salt flat margins hold Tawny Pipit and Calandra Lark. In autumn, the lagoon attracts migrant waders in numbers, with Curlew Sandpiper, Little Stint, and Broad-billed Sandpiper all recorded regularly. The adjacent steppe grassland adds Corn Bunting and Quail.

## 10. Medobory Nature Reserve, Ternopil Oblast

Medobory in the Podilia Uplands of western Ukraine protects a ridge of forested limestone outcrops in an agricultural landscape, creating island habitats for forest species in otherwise open country. The reserve is best known for its mammals, including Roe Deer, Red Fox, and Wild Boar, but the bird community is excellent. White Stork nests on the farmland around the reserve perimeter. The forest interior has Collared Flycatcher — one of Europe's most ornately marked small birds, with a bold white collar and forehead — breeding in the deciduous woodland alongside Pied Flycatcher. The limestone outcrops hold Black Redstart and Peregrine Falcon, and the reserve's mosaic of habitats gives it exceptional species diversity relative to its small area.
